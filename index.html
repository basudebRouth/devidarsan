    Significance of Durga Puja
    
As per Hindu mythology, the festival marks Goddess Durga's visit to her natal 
home along with her children.
After the ceremonit of al bath, the tree or "Kola Bou" (as caled in Bengali)
is draped in a saree, usually a red-bordered one, and 
placed on the right-side of Ganesha, thereby implying that
 "Kola Bou" is actually the bride of Ganesha.
However, several cultural revisionists and historians have 
different views on this as some believe that 
"Kola Bou" is another representation of Durga. 
Hence, they refute the theory that "Kola Bou" is the bride of Ganesha. 
Another different view is that "Kola Bou" is a symbolic representation
of nine types of plants that form a sacred complex. 
Priests tie a bunch of eight plants on the trunk of 
the banyan tree before performing the ritual. 
The nine different leaves combined together form 
"Kola Bou"often considered as the plant form of Durga.
  

    How is Durga Puja celebrated?
    
Schools, colleges, and government offices are closed during the puja. 
People indulge in shopping for the puja just after Mahalaya.
During this time of the year, most shops offer discounts
on clothes and other products.On "Maha Ashtami" menusually 
wear Kurta Pajamas while women drape themselves in sarees. 
Other than the celebrations, the festival calls for a family get-together.
s It's a time when people bridge their differences and come together to celebrate unity.
To be precise,DurgaPuja transcends 
the boundaries of religion and celebrates the spirit of humanity.
